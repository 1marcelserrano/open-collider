# Worked example — personal brand × distant domains

A full open-collider run, start to finish. Read it to see what the skill
actually produces: a domain bank with named references, one isolated
collision, the cross-collision, the ranked pool, and the falsifier checks.

This is a demonstration run, lightly trimmed for length (the live skill
generates 15–20 ideas per collision; here we keep the strongest from each).

---

## Stage 1 — Briefing

- **Ideation problem:** original angles for an essay on building a personal brand without sounding like everyone else.
- **"Good idea" criterion:** the angle reveals a *mechanism*, not a vibe — something you could build a section around. Reusable past one essay.
- **Non-negotiable constraints:** no "be authentic" platitudes; no growth-hacking tactics; nothing that only works for people who are already famous.
- **Output destination:** a long-form essay for a writer's newsletter.
- **Desired volume:** ~8 curated angles.

## Stage 2 — Domain bank (6 distant domains)

Each domain carries a **specific named reference** — the rule that turns
decorative analogy into operational mechanism.

**DOMAIN 1 — Mycology: *Armillaria ostoyae*, the "humongous fungus" (Malheur National Forest, Oregon; mapped 1998, ~9 km²).**
- Counterintuitive mechanism: the organism is almost entirely an underground mycelial network. The visible mushrooms are a tiny, seasonal fraction of a vast, patient, mostly-invisible body.
- Bridge question: what if a brand's visible output is the fruiting body, and the real organism is the underground network nobody sees?

**DOMAIN 2 — Lighthouse optics: the Fresnel lens (Augustin-Jean Fresnel, 1822).**
- Counterintuitive mechanism: concentric prism rings bend scattered light into one coherent beam visible 30+ km out — reach by *concentration*, not by adding wattage.
- Bridge question: what if being seen at a distance is an optics problem, not a volume problem?

**DOMAIN 3 — Sourdough fermentation: Chad Robertson's Tartine starter.**
- Counterintuitive mechanism: a living culture you feed on a schedule. Flavor is a function of *time and neglect-resistance*, not of any single bake. A starter can survive being ignored and revived.
- Bridge question: what if a brand is a culture you feed daily, where the flavor only develops over time you can't shortcut?

**DOMAIN 4 — Heraldry: medieval coats of arms, England's College of Arms (chartered 1484).**
- Counterintuitive mechanism: a fixed visual grammar (tinctures, ordinaries, charges) encodes identity legibly across distance and illiteracy. The constraint is the feature — a rigid system makes you instantly recognizable.
- Bridge question: what if recognizability comes from a *self-imposed grammar*, not from constant novelty?

**DOMAIN 5 — Free-diving: Herbert Nitsch, "No Limits" record holder (214 m).**
- Counterintuitive mechanism: the mammalian dive reflex — you go deeper by slowing the heart and spending less, not by adding effort. Panic (effort) kills; calm (subtraction) extends range.
- Bridge question: what if reach comes from doing dramatically less, on purpose?

**DOMAIN 6 — Modal jazz: Miles Davis, *Kind of Blue* (1959).**
- Counterintuitive mechanism: fewer chords, more space. By stripping the harmonic grid to a couple of modes, each player's voice becomes unmistakable. Constraint manufactures identity.
- Bridge question: what if a recognizable voice is a function of how much you *remove*?

> Distance check: microbiology × optics × fermentation × medieval bureaucracy × physiology × music. Different vocabularies, eras, time-scales, disciplines. No two are "close cousins."

## Stage 3 — Isolated collision (one shown in full)

```
=== COLLISION: personal brand × Fresnel lens (1822) ===

HIDDEN DIMENSION IDENTIFIED:
A brand and a lighthouse solve the same problem — being seen at a distance
on very little energy. The Fresnel lens does it by concentrating scattered
light into a single coherent beam. The amateur move is to add wattage (post
more, shout louder). The Fresnel move is to concentrate what's already there.

IDEAS (strongest kept):
1. The editorial Fresnel lens — take twenty lukewarm opinions you hold and
   bend them all into a single beam of thesis. The brightness comes from
   concentration, not volume. An essay section: "audit your scatter."
2. Beam vs. floodlight as a positioning choice — most personal brands floodlight
   (illuminate everything dimly). A beam is dark almost everywhere and blinding
   on one bearing. Pick the bearing.
3. The "characteristic" of a lighthouse is its unique blink pattern, so sailors
   tell them apart at night. Your brand's characteristic isn't your topic —
   it's the *rhythm and signature* by which people recognize you with the sound off.
4. Range over reach — a lighthouse isn't trying to be seen by everyone, only by
   the ships in its waters. Define your waters before your wattage.
```

(The other five domains each produce their own block in a live run. Mycology
yields the "underground network is the real organism" line of ideas; modal jazz
yields the "voice = what you remove" cluster; etc.)

## Stage 3.5 — Cross-collision

The two strongest single-domain collisions were **Fresnel lens** (concentration)
and **heraldry** (fixed grammar). Cross them with each other, then back to the brief.

```
=== CROSS-COLLISION: Fresnel lens × heraldry → personal brand ===

TRIPLE DIMENSION:
Fresnel = concentrate scattered signal into one beam.
Heraldry = a fixed grammar that stays legible across distance and noise.
Both, against the brief: a minimal, self-imposed system that is recognizable
at a distance AND concentrates the message — identity as a constrained optics.

IDEAS:
1. Build a personal "coat of arms" — three fixed elements (a recurring claim,
   a recurring form, a recurring enemy) you never vary, so you stay legible
   even when the topic changes. The grammar is the brand; the posts are weather.
2. The blazon test — heralds could describe any coat of arms in one precise
   sentence of fixed vocabulary. If a stranger can't "blazon" you in one
   sentence after three posts, your signal is scattered, not concentrated.
```

## Stage 4 — Curation (3 filters applied)

- **Filter 1 (signal vs. noise):** dropped two ideas that just restated the
  domain with no real bridge (e.g. "be a lighthouse for your audience" — vibe, no mechanism).
- **Filter 2 (voice/style fit):** rewrote "audit your scatter" inline from the
  clunkier "do a brand-light-concentration audit." Flagged the blazon test as
  structurally strong but needing a worked demo in the essay.
- **Filter 3 (brief criterion):** kept only angles that reveal a reusable
  mechanism and survive the "no platitudes / no growth-hacking / works for
  non-famous people" constraints.

## Stage 4 falsifiers (run before delivery)

- **Test A vs B** — drop the domain bank, use only the brief: would I reach these? *No.* Angles 1–8 each depend on the bridge. Pass.
- **Test C** — do the ideas depend on the bridge, or would "be original" reach them? *Depend on the bridge.* "Voice as subtraction" comes from modal jazz, not from a generic originality prompt. Pass.
- **Test D** — would a 3× longer, more detailed brief (no domains) reach here? *No.* More brief detail pulls toward the center, not toward Oregon fungus. Pass.

All three pass. No rerun needed.
